3E 5 Mainly minerals

The term mineral has two different meanings in science. When speaking of rocks, a mineral is a pure substance that crystallises out of a rock. Such pure substances may be elements or compounds. When speaking of nutrients, a mineral is an element required by a living organism in some form – that form is almost always either a compound or an ion.
  1. Fluorapatite is a mineral found in rock phosphate, used to make the fertiliser superphosphate. The chemical formula for fluorapatite is Ca5(PO4)3F.
    1. How many different kinds of atoms are there in fluorapatite?

      4
    2. How many atoms in total are in the above formula for fluorapatite?

      21
  2. Phosphorus, nitrogen and potassium are the most important minerals for plant growth.
    1. Write the electron arrangement for the phosphorus atom (15P).

      2 . 8 . 5
    2. Write the symbol for the phosphide ion.

      P3–
    3. What group of the periodic table does nitrogen belong to?

      15
    4. Plants absorb nitrogen in the form of nitrates. Write the formula for the nitrate ion.

      NO3–
    5. Explain by referring to its electron structure why the element potassium is violently reactive.

      Potassium's electron structure is 2.8.8.1. If it loses one electron it will have a full outer shell of electrons, which is a stable arrangement.
      Potassium is violently reactive because it is easy to lose the outer shell electron.
  3. Scientists study the way plants use phosphorus by applying a fertiliser containing an isotope of phosphorus with 17 neutrons.
    1. What are isotopes?

      Atoms with same number of protons (or same atomic number) but different numbers of neutrons (or different mass numbers).
    2. Write the atomic symbol for this isotope of phosphorus.

      32P
