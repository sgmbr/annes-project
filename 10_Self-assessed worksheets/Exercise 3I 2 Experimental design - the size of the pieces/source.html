3I 2 Experimental design: the size of the pieces

Limestone is a versatile material with many different uses. It can be purchased as large blocks or bricks, as fragments ranging from thumb size to tiny grains, and even as fine powder. Limeworks use sieves of different sizes to sort crushed limestone into the various grades.

You have been given jars of limestone fragments of 2.5 mm, 2.0 mm, 1.5 mm, 1.0 mm and 0.5 mm size. You are to design an experiment to investigate the way the size of the pieces affects the rate of reaction between limestone and dilute hydrochloric acid.

  1. Write a word equation for the reaction between limestone (calcium carbonate) and hydrochloric acid.
    (Copy and paste this arrow character (→) or type an arrow with a computer keyboard using Alt 26.)

    carbonate + hydrochloric acid → calcium chloride + water + carbon dioxide
  2. What method will you use to compare the rate of reaction of the different-sized pieces of limestone?

    The volume of gas OR the number of bubbles being produced in a stated time (eg 30 s).

  3. What variable will you change during this experiment?

    The size of the pieces.

  4. What variables will you need to keep the same during this experiment?

    The mass of calcium carbonate/chips used; the volume of acid used; the concentration of acid used; the temperature of the solutions; the way the volume of gas is measured; the way the time is measured. One tick per variable up to 3 ticks.

  5. What should you do to be certain of your answer?

    Repeat each trial at least 3 times.

  6. Write a detailed plan for this experiment.

    Weigh out 3 g (state a mass) of each of the different-sized limestone chips.
    Measure out exactly 10 mL (state a volume) of dilute hydrochloric acid
    (use the same bottle/concentration of acid each time),
    then quickly add the limestone, stopper and collect the gas under water into a measuring cylinder. Measure and record the volume of gas produced in exactly 30 s (a stated time).
    Repeat 5 times for each size of limestone chips.
    One tick each underlined point up to 4 ticks.

  7. What would you expect to find out if this experiment was done?

    As the size of the pieces increases the rate of reaction decreases.

  8. Explain the scientific principle behind your answer to question 7 above.

    The larger the size of each piece of limestone, the fewer the number of pieces needed for a given mass of limestone and the smaller the total surface area of those pieces. With a lower surface area there is less limestone exposed to the acid and fewer collisions between acid and limestone particles, leading to a slower reaction rate.
    Links larger particles to a lower total surface area (or smaller particles to a greater total surface area).
    Links total surface area to number of collisions between acid and limestone particles.
    Links number of collisions to reaction rate.
