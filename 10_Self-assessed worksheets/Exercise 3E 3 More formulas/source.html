  4. Explain why the correct formula for calcium chloride is CaCl2.

    Calcium has 2 outer-shell electrons, which it loses to form the ion Ca2+. Chlorine has 7 outer-shell electrons, so it gains 1 electrons to form Cl–. In this way both ions have full outer-shells of electrons. In the neutral compound calcium chloride, the number of positive and negative charges must be equal. This is done by having twice as many Cl– ions as Ca2+ ions, leading to the formula CaCl2.
