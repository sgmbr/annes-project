3H 3 Indigestion

Cells in your stomach release hydrochloric acid to help break down carbohydrate and protein molecules. Indigestion happens when there is too much acid produced. The sufferer feels pain in the stomach or upper chest. Most indigestion remedies work by neutralising the excess stomach acid.

  3. Sodium hydroxide is a cheap, readily available compound that neutralises hydrochloric acid. Why is sodium hydroxide never found in indigestion remedies?

    Sodium hydroxide is a very basic and caustic substance. If it is ingested it would be very corrosive and cause serious damage to lips, mouth and oesophagus before reaching the acid in the stomach. Hence it is not used in indigestion remedies.
