3G 6 Reaction time

    1. Name the acid that reacts with magnesium hydroxide to form magnesium sulfate.

      sulfuric acid
    2. What gas is formed when lithium carbonate reacts with hydrochloric acid?

      carbon dioxide
    3. Identify the salt formed when calcium oxide reacts with sulfuric acid.

      calcium sulfate
    4. What substance reacts with nitric acid to form zinc nitrate and carbon dioxide gas?

      zinc carbonate
    5. State the oxide that reacts with hydrochloric acid to form zinc chloride.

      zinc oxide

  2. Melanie added black copper oxide powder to sulfuric acid and heated it. The colour of the liquid turned blue. Then she filtered the mixture, heated the filtrate until it was nearly dry, and left it overnight.
    1. Name the blue compound.

      copper sulfate
    2. What colour solid would she see on the filter paper?

      black
    3. The photo on the right shows the evaporating dish the next day. Identify the blue crystals.

      copper sulfate
    4. Write a balanced formula equation for the reaction between copper oxide and sulfuric acid.
      (Copy and paste this arrow character (→) or type an arrow with a computer keyboard using Alt 26.)

      CuO + H2SO4 → CuSO4 + H2O

  3. Lance took a small amount of magnesium carbonate and added it to a beaker containing a lot of dilute nitric acid. A reaction occurred.
    1. What would he observe during this reaction?

      The powder would fizz because a gas is produced. The powder would eventually disappear.
    2. Write a word equation for the reaction that occured.

      magnesium carbonate + nitric acid → magnesium nitrate + carbon dioxide + water
    3. Write a balanced formula equation for the reaction.

      MgCO3 + 2HNO3 → Mg(NO3)2 + CO2 + H2O
      4 formulas correct.
      Equation fully correct and balanced.
    4. He put a small amount of the solution remaining in the beaker into a test tube and added a few drops of universal indicator solution. What would he see and why?

      The indicator will turn red. A small of carbonate was added to a lot of acid. After all the powder has reacted there will be unreacted acid left in the beaker. Thus the indicator will turn red because of the acid in the beaker.
      States that the indicator or solution is red.
      Recognises that there is unreacted acid left in the beaker.
