  3. A home ‘handy-hints’ book says that rust stains on clothes can be removed by soaking the stain in vinegar or lemon juice, or by rubbing a paste of cream of tartar in water on the stain.
    1. What do these three substances have in common?

      They are all acidic.
    2. Why would these substances remove the rust stain?

      The rust stain is iron oxide — a metal oxide. Acids react with metal oxides to form salts and water. If the salt is soluble in water it will dissolve, and in this way the rust is removed.
      Recognises that rust is a metal oxide.
      Recognises that the reaction with acid will form a salt that dissolves.
